Planning Your Trip

When to Visit Toronto

Toronto has four proper seasons, each with its own character. 2026 is an unusual year — the FIFA World Cup window (June–July) will make summer busier and more expensive than normal.

Winter
Dec – Feb · −10°C to 0°C

Cold. The Distillery Christmas Market and Nathan Phillips Square ice rink are highlights. Lowest hotel rates. Indoor culture — ROM, AGO, Hockey Hall of Fame — comes into its own.

Spring
Mar – May · 5°C to 18°C

Toronto wakes up. Cherry blossoms in High Park draw crowds in late April. Shoulder-season pricing before summer peaks. Toronto Maple Leafs playoffs drive hotel spikes if the team runs deep.

Summer ★
Jun – Aug · 20°C to 30°C

Peak season. Waterfront opens up, festivals run constantly, the city is at maximum energy. FIFA World Cup in June, Toronto Pride late June, CNE in August. Book well ahead — especially in 2026.

Autumn
Sep – Nov · 5°C to 18°C

The sweet spot. Fall colours in the ravines and High Park are stunning. Post-summer pricing, comfortable temperatures, the Toronto International Film Festival in September, and the city to yourself relative to peak.

Getting Around

Transport in Toronto

Toronto is large but navigable. The TTC covers most tourist areas well. The single most useful purchase is a PRESTO card — works across TTC and GO Transit and saves you the hassle of exact change.

🚇
TTC Subway & Streetcar

Toronto's primary transit network. Four subway lines plus an extensive streetcar network (particularly useful downtown on King and Queen Streets). PRESTO card is cheapest. Single fare $3.30 CAD.

🚆
UP Express — Airport Train

Pearson Airport to Union Station in 25 minutes. Departs every 15 minutes. Far superior to a taxi or rideshare in traffic. $9.10 with PRESTO, $12.35 with a credit card. The right way to arrive in Toronto.

🚌
GO Transit — Regional Rail

Connects Toronto to Hamilton, Niagara Falls, Barrie, and surrounding commuter cities. Essential for day trips to Niagara (approx. 2 hrs). PRESTO card works on GO. Book in advance for popular routes.

🚇
Eglinton Crosstown LRT (2026)

Toronto's long-awaited east-west rapid transit corridor opens in 2026. 19km connecting Scarborough to Mount Dennis. Transforms transit access to midtown including the AGO neighbourhood.

🚗
Rideshare (Uber / Lyft)

Widely available and affordable for cross-town trips. Avoid downtown core during rush hour — traffic is severe. Useful for late-night returns and airport runs if UP Express timing doesn't work.

⛴
Toronto Island Ferry

A $9 CAD return ferry from Jack Layton Ferry Terminal takes you to Toronto Island Park — 560 acres of green space and beaches with spectacular skyline views back at the city. Summer only (May–October peak).

City Anatomy

Understanding Toronto's Neighbourhoods

Downtown & Entertainment District

The CN Tower, Ripley's Aquarium, Rogers Centre, and Scotiabank Arena anchor this dense, tourist-heavy zone. Hotel options range from luxury (Ritz-Carlton, St. Regis) to mid-range. Best base for short stays or those focused on major attractions and sports events.

Yorkville

Toronto's upscale village. Boutiques, galleries, the ROM, and the city's best concentration of fine dining and luxury hotels (Four Seasons, Hazelton, Kimpton). Quieter than downtown — the right choice for leisure-focused visitors who want refinement over convenience.

The Annex

University of Toronto's neighbourhood — bookshops, cafes, the ROM, and the kind of intellectual energy that cities pay millions to cultivate. Bloor Street's retail corridor connects The Annex to Yorkville heading east.

Kensington Market & Chinatown

The most concentrated expression of Toronto's diversity. Vintage shops, international food stalls, independent roasters, and the AGO nearby. Chaotic, vibrant, and completely authentic. The Ace Hotel is here for visitors who want to feel it, not just observe it.

Distillery District

Perfectly preserved Victorian industrial architecture converted into one of North America's best arts and entertainment precincts. Galleries, boutiques, award-winning restaurants, and the city's best Christmas Market (December). A must-visit regardless of your interests.

The Waterfront

Lake Ontario's north shore — 46km of continuous waterfront parks, trails, and marinas. Toronto Islands are accessible by a 10-minute ferry. Best in summer. Hotel X Toronto and 1 Hotel are the standout accommodation options in this zone.
