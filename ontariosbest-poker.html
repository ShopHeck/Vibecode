Ontario's Poker Market

What Makes Ontario's Poker Scene Unique

Ontario is the only province in Canada with a regulated online poker market. Since the AGCO launched its iGaming framework on April 4, 2022, six major poker rooms have obtained licences and operate legally — but with a critical constraint: ring-fenced liquidity. You can only play against other players physically located in Ontario, not global pools.

That means smaller player pools than the international versions of these same sites. But it also means full regulatory protection: segregated player funds, certified RNG software, mandatory responsible gambling tools, and formal dispute resolution through the AGCO.

⚖️

Shared Liquidity: A Ruling to Watch

In November 2025, a Superior Court of Ontario ruled that shared liquidity between Ontario and other jurisdictions is legal under current law, potentially opening Ontario's player pool to international markets. That ruling is currently under appeal — a decision is expected mid-2026. If upheld, it could transform Ontario's online poker landscape entirely. We'll update this page when a ruling is issued.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online poker legal in Ontario?+
Yes. Online poker has been legal and regulated in Ontario since April 4, 2022. Only sites licensed by the AGCO and registered with iGaming Ontario may legally offer real-money poker to Ontario residents. Six rooms currently hold active licences. Playing at unlicensed offshore sites is technically in a grey area — iGaming Ontario advises players to "make the safer choice" and stick to regulated rooms.
Why can I only play against Ontario players?+
Ontario's AGCO licence requires all poker rooms to operate in a ring-fenced model — meaning you can only play against other players physically located within Ontario's borders. This is enforced through mandatory geolocation technology. A November 2025 Superior Court ruling found that international shared liquidity may be permissible under current law, but that decision is under appeal. If upheld mid-2026, Ontario could become the first jurisdiction in North America to permit formal cross-border poker liquidity.
Are poker winnings taxable in Ontario?+
For most recreational players, no. The CRA does not treat gambling winnings as taxable income for casual players. Taxation applies only when poker is your primary profession — meaning it's structured, deliberate, and your main income source. The vast majority of Ontario poker players have no tax obligation. Consult a tax professional if you're a high-stakes or full-time player.
Which room has the most traffic?+
GGPoker Ontario leads with approximately 57% of Ontario's cash game market and 350–400 active seats during peak hours (8–11 PM ET). PokerStars Ontario is second. BetMGM Poker, PartyPoker Ontario, and Bwin share a player pool on the PartyPoker network, making their combined liquidity competitive. Traffic data is updated monthly — we track peak-hour seat counts across all six rooms.
Can I play on global PokerStars or GGPoker from Ontario?+
No. Ontario residents must use the Ontario-specific versions of these platforms — PokerStars Ontario and GGPoker Ontario — which operate on separate, ring-fenced player pools. The global ".com" versions will detect your Ontario location and redirect or block you. Each Ontario room has its own distinct software client, account system, and player pool that is entirely separate from international play.
What is the best room for beginners?+
888poker Ontario consistently offers the softest player competition in the province, making it the top choice for recreational and beginning players. GGPoker Ontario has the most traffic (more tables available at all stakes) and the best learning tools through PokerCraft. PokerStars Ontario has the strongest freeroll and low-stakes tournament programme for players building a bankroll from scratch.
