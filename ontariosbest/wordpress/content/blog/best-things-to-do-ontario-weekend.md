<p>Ontario is the most visited province in Canada for good reason. It's home to one of the world's most famous natural wonders, Canada's largest city, a stunning national park system, a world-class wine region, and more festivals, events, and attractions than any weekend could hold. Whether you're looking for a day trip from Toronto, a long weekend in cottage country, or things to do in Ottawa, this guide covers the best Ontario has to offer in 2026.</p>

<h2>Niagara Falls — Ontario's #1 Attraction</h2>

<p>Niagara Falls remains Ontario's single most visited destination — and rightly so. The Canadian Horseshoe Falls is more than twice as wide as the American falls, and the viewing experience from the Ontario side is dramatically better. Go early in the morning on a weekday to avoid crowds and get the best light for photos. Must-dos at Niagara beyond the falls themselves:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Journey Behind the Falls</strong> — tunnels carved into the rock directly behind the Horseshoe Falls</li>
<li><strong>Hornblower Niagara Cruises</strong> — get close enough to feel the mist</li>
<li><strong>Niagara-on-the-Lake</strong> — 20 minutes from the falls, one of the most charming towns in Canada with the Shaw Festival theatre and dozens of wineries on the Niagara wine route</li>
<li><strong>Clifton Hill</strong> — entertainment strip with attractions, restaurants, and casinos for an evening out</li>
</ul>

<p>Getting there: 1.5 hours from Toronto via QEW. No car? GO Bus service runs from Toronto's Union Station.</p>

<h2>Algonquin Provincial Park — Ontario's Wild Heart</h2>

<p>Just 3–4 hours from Toronto, Algonquin Park is one of Canada's most iconic wilderness destinations. The park covers 7,725 square kilometres of forests, lakes, and rivers — home to moose, black bears, wolves, and some of the best canoe routes on the continent. Weekend options range from car camping at Mew Lake Campground to multi-day canoe portage trips through the interior. The fall colours in Algonquin (mid-September through mid-October) are among the most spectacular anywhere in North America.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Spring/Summer:</strong> Canoe the Oxtongue River, hike the Centennial Ridges Trail, spot moose along Highway 60</li>
<li><strong>Fall:</strong> Drive the 56km Highway 60 Corridor for peak foliage, visit the Algonquin Art Centre</li>
<li><strong>Winter:</strong> Cross-country skiing on groomed trails, snowshoeing, winter camping for the adventurous</li>
</ul>

<h2>Muskoka — Ontario's Cottage Country</h2>

<p>The Muskoka region — roughly 2 hours north of Toronto — is synonymous with Canadian summer. Lakes Muskoka, Rosseau, and Joseph sit at the heart of a region packed with luxury resorts, boutique inns, waterfront dining, and small-town character. Key Muskoka towns to explore:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Gravenhurst</strong> — home of Muskoka Wharf, the birthplace of Norman Bethune, and excellent Muskoka Brewery</li>
<li><strong>Bracebridge</strong> — Santa's Village (family favourite), beautiful Bracebridge Falls, Dunn's Pavilion summer concerts</li>
<li><strong>Port Carling</strong> — the "Hub of the Lakes," excellent boutique shopping and waterfront dining</li>
<li><strong>Huntsville</strong> — gateway to Algonquin, host of the past G8 Summit, vibrant arts scene</li>
</ul>

<h2>Toronto — More Than a Day Can Handle</h2>

<p>Toronto is a full destination in itself. Highlights for a weekend:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Kensington Market & Chinatown</strong> — arguably the most eclectic neighbourhood in Canada; the best street food per square block anywhere in Ontario</li>
<li><strong>St. Lawrence Market</strong> — one of the world's great food markets; Saturday morning is peak time</li>
<li><strong>Distillery District</strong> — Victorian industrial architecture turned arts and dining hub; excellent brunch, galleries, and boutiques</li>
<li><strong>Royal Ontario Museum (ROM)</strong> — world-class natural history and culture; the Michael Lee-Chin Crystal addition alone is worth a visit</li>
<li><strong>CN Tower EdgeWalk</strong> — walk hands-free around the outside of the CN Tower's main pod, 356 metres above the city. Not for the faint of heart.</li>
<li><strong>Ripley's Aquarium of Canada</strong> — spectacular and genuinely excellent for all ages; the shark tunnel is memorable</li>
</ul>

<p>See our <a href="/restaurants/">Restaurants directory</a> for reviewed Toronto dining options across every cuisine and price point.</p>

<h2>Ottawa — Canada's Capital Worth Exploring</h2>

<p>Ottawa is frequently underrated as a weekend destination. It's a genuinely beautiful city, especially in summer when the Rideau Canal is in full use, and it has a concentration of world-class free museums that would embarrass larger capitals:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Canadian Museum of History</strong> (Gatineau, across the river) — the most visited museum in Canada</li>
<li><strong>National Gallery of Canada</strong> — outstanding collection in a landmark Moshe Safdie building</li>
<li><strong>Rideau Canal</strong> — paddle in summer, skate in winter on the world's largest naturally frozen skating rink</li>
<li><strong>ByWard Market</strong> — the city's historic market neighbourhood, excellent for brunch, local producers, and nightlife</li>
<li><strong>Parliament Hill</strong> — free tours of the Centre Block (book ahead), spectacular setting above the Ottawa River</li>
</ul>

<h2>Prince Edward County — Ontario's Wine & Culinary Destination</h2>

<p>Prince Edward County (PEC) — 2.5 hours east of Toronto — has transformed over the past decade into Ontario's premier culinary and wine destination. The county's limestone-rich soil produces exceptional Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, and the restaurant scene has followed: award-winning restaurants occupy converted heritage buildings across Picton, Wellington, and Bloomfield. Sandbanks Provincial Park offers the largest freshwater sand dunes in the world. Combine a winery tour, a beach day, and dinner at a county restaurant for the definitive PEC weekend.</p>

<h2>Practical Tips for Ontario Weekend Travel</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>Book accommodations well ahead</strong> for summer and fall — popular destinations like Niagara, Muskoka, and PEC sell out months in advance</li>
<li><strong>Provincial parks reservations</strong> open at 8am on specific dates via Ontario Parks — set a calendar reminder or campsites will be gone</li>
<li><strong>GO Transit</strong> connects Toronto to Niagara Falls, Barrie, Oshawa, and Hamilton — a car isn't required for many popular day trips</li>
<li><strong>Check Ontario Travel</strong> (ontario.ca/travel) for current events, festivals, and attraction hours</li>
</ul>
