Need to Know

Ontario's iGaming Regulation Explained

Ontario is the only Canadian province with a fully developed private-sector iGaming market. Understanding how it works helps you make smarter choices about where to play.

The market operates under a two-body structure: the Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario (AGCO) sets and enforces the rules — including standards for game fairness, data security, and responsible gambling. iGaming Ontario (iGO), now an independent Crown agency since May 2025 under Bill 216, manages operating agreements with private casino operators.

For players, what this means in practice is that every licensed casino must: keep your funds in segregated accounts separate from company operating funds; use independently audited random number generators; provide mandatory responsible gambling tools; and offer regulated dispute resolution if something goes wrong. These are legal requirements, not optional features.

Offshore sites operating without AGCO registration offer none of these protections. The choice between a licensed and unlicensed site is not just a regulatory technicality — it's the difference between having legal recourse and having none.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online gambling legal in Ontario? +
Yes. Ontario launched its regulated online gambling market on April 4, 2022 — the first province in Canada to do so. Private operators can legally offer online casino games, sports betting, and poker to Ontario residents, provided they are licensed by the AGCO and registered with iGaming Ontario. Playing at an AGCO-licensed site is fully legal for Ontario residents aged 19 and over.
What is the legal gambling age in Ontario? +
The legal online gambling age in Ontario is 19. All AGCO-licensed casinos are required to verify your age and identity through KYC (Know Your Customer) checks before you can make a withdrawal. There are no exceptions — this is a hard regulatory requirement, not operator policy.
Are casino winnings taxable in Ontario? +
Generally, no. Gambling winnings are not taxable for casual players in Canada. The CRA does not consider gambling income taxable unless you are a professional gambler — defined as someone for whom gambling is a primary and structured income source. For the vast majority of players, winnings from licensed Ontario online casinos are tax-free. Consult a tax professional if gambling represents a significant portion of your income.
How do I verify a casino is legally licensed in Ontario? +
Look for the iGaming Ontario and AGCO logos in the site's footer. You can also cross-reference the official AGCO registry and iGaming Ontario's operator directory to confirm any site's current active status. Can I use Interac at Ontario online casinos? +
Yes. Interac is the most widely supported payment method at AGCO-licensed casinos and is the fastest option for both deposits and withdrawals for Canadian players. Interac e-Transfer withdrawals typically process within 24–72 hours depending on the operator, with Horseshoe Casino currently offering the fastest processing in our testing. What responsible gambling tools are available? +
All AGCO-licensed casinos are required by law to offer deposit limits, loss limits, wagering limits, session time reminders, and self-exclusion options. Ontario also operates the My PlayBreak program, which allows players to self-exclude from all licensed Ontario gambling sites simultaneously. These tools are accessible directly from your account settings on any licensed platform.
