Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is sports betting legal in Ontario?+
Yes. Online sports betting has been fully legal in Ontario since April 4, 2022. Operators must be AGCO-licensed and registered with iGaming Ontario. You must be 19 or older and physically located within Ontario's borders to bet legally.
How many licensed sportsbooks operate in Ontario?+
As of March 2026, 35 sportsbooks hold active AGCO licences in Ontario, including STX (a betting exchange) and PROLINE+, operated by the OLG. You can verify the current full list directly at the iGaming Ontario operator directory.
Are sports betting winnings taxable in Ontario?+
For recreational bettors, no. Gambling winnings are not considered taxable income in Canada. The CRA only taxes gambling income when betting is your primary profession structured as a business activity. The vast majority of Ontario sports bettors have no tax obligation on winnings. Consult a tax professional if you're a high-volume bettor.
Can I use Interac at Ontario sportsbooks?+
Yes. Interac e-Transfer is the most widely supported payment method at AGCO-licensed sportsbooks and the fastest for withdrawals. Note that crypto deposits and withdrawals are not permitted at any regulated Ontario sportsbook — all transactions must be in CAD.
Can Ontario sportsbooks advertise promotions?+
No — not publicly. AGCO regulations prohibit licensed sportsbooks from advertising bonuses, free bets, or promotional offers in public-facing marketing. Operators may communicate offers directly to registered, consenting players within their own platform. This is why you won't see bonus comparisons on this site.
