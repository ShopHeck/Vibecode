Insider Advice

Booking Tips for Ontario

01
Book FIFA World Cup dates now

Toronto hosts six FIFA World Cup 2026 matches between June 12 and July 2. Hotel rates during this window are already surging. If your travel overlaps, book immediately — the best properties will sell out entirely.

02
Yorkville vs Downtown — know the difference

Yorkville (Four Seasons, Hazelton, Kimpton) is quieter, more refined, and ideal for leisure. Downtown/Entertainment District (Ritz, St. Regis) puts you in the middle of everything — better for short stays and business.

03
Waterfront hotels book out for CNE

The Canadian National Exhibition runs August 21 – September 7, drawing 1.4 million visitors. Waterfront hotels (1 Hotel, Hotel X) fill up weeks in advance for this window.

04
Check MLSE event calendars

Maple Leafs playoffs and Raptors home games spike hotel rates in the Entertainment District dramatically. Check the Scotiabank Arena schedule before booking — or use it to explain the price you're seeing.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best area to stay in Toronto?+
It depends on your priorities. Downtown Core and the Entertainment District offer the most central location with easy access to major attractions, restaurants, and Scotiabank Arena. Yorkville is quieter, more upscale, and ideal for leisure travel with proximity to the ROM and Bloor Street boutiques. The Waterfront is excellent for summer visits — lakefront views, easy access to the island ferry, and proximity to major venues. For first-time visitors, Downtown Core is usually the best default.
When is the most expensive time to visit Toronto?+
2026 is an unusually expensive year. FIFA World Cup matches (June 12 – July 2), Toronto Pride (June 25–28), and the CNE (August 21 – September 7) all drive significant demand spikes. Outside of those windows, summer weekends are generally the most expensive. January and February offer the lowest rates but also the coldest weather. The sweet spot for value is late September through October — fall colours, comfortable temperatures, and post-summer pricing.
Are there good hotels outside Toronto in Ontario?+
Absolutely. Niagara-on-the-Lake has some of Ontario's most charming boutique inns and wine country retreats — The Prince of Wales Hotel is a perennial favourite. Ottawa's Fairmont Château Laurier is a national landmark. Muskoka cottage country has world-class lake resorts including the JW Marriott The Rosseau. Our full Ontario hotels guide covers all regions — this page focuses on Toronto specifically.
What booking platform offers the best rates?+
It varies. Booking.com and Hotels.com tend to have the widest selection and competitive base rates. Expedia's bundle deals (hotel + flight) can save significantly on the total trip cost. Luxury properties like the St. Regis and Four Seasons often have their own best-rate guarantees when booking direct — worth checking before committing to a third party.
