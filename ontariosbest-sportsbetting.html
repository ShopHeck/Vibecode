Regulation

How Legal Sports Betting Works in Ontario

Single-event sports betting was illegal across Canada until the federal government passed Bill C-218 in June 2021, which amended the Criminal Code to permit provinces to regulate it. Ontario moved first, launching its private-operator market on April 4, 2022.

The market is governed by two bodies. The AGCO issues operator licenses and enforces the Registrar's Standards — including rules on integrity, advertising, and responsible gambling. iGaming Ontario (iGO), now an independent Crown agency since May 2025, manages the operating agreements with each private sportsbook and ensures they conduct gambling on behalf of the province.

For bettors, the practical effect is: your funds are held in segregated accounts, disputes have a formal regulatory channel, problem gambling tools are mandatory, and all odds are generated from certified, audited software. Offshore sportsbooks offer none of these protections.

You must be physically located within Ontario's borders to place bets — geolocation technology is a licensing requirement and actively enforced.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is sports betting legal in Ontario?+
Yes. Online sports betting has been fully legal in Ontario since April 4, 2022. Operators must be licensed by the AGCO and registered with iGaming Ontario. You must be 19 or older and physically located within Ontario to place bets at a regulated sportsbook.
How many licensed sportsbooks are in Ontario?+
As of March 2026, there are 35 sportsbooks licensed by the AGCO and operating legally in Ontario. This includes STX, a betting exchange that allows bettors to buy and sell positions on sporting outcomes, and PROLINE+, operated by the Ontario Lottery and Gaming Corporation. You can verify the current list directly at the iGaming Ontario operator directory.
Are sports betting winnings taxable in Ontario?+
For recreational bettors, no. Gambling winnings are not considered taxable income in Canada under the Income Tax Act. The CRA only taxes gambling income in specific circumstances — primarily when betting is your primary profession and constitutes a structured business activity. The vast majority of Ontario sports bettors have no tax obligation on their winnings. If you're a high-volume bettor, consult a tax professional.
Can I use Interac at Ontario sportsbooks?+
Yes. Interac e-Transfer is the most widely supported Canadian payment method at AGCO-licensed sportsbooks and the most reliable for withdrawals. Deposits are typically instant. Withdrawal processing times vary by operator — bet365 is currently the fastest in our testing, with funds often arriving within hours. Note that crypto deposits and withdrawals are not permitted at any regulated Ontario sportsbook.
Can I bet on political events in Ontario?+
Yes. Political betting is legal at Ontario-licensed sportsbooks, and most major operators offer election and political event markets. The AGCO permits wagering on outcomes that are verifiable and where the event is not deemed offensive or unethical. This is distinct from prediction markets, which currently operate in a different regulatory category in Ontario — see our prediction markets page for the full breakdown.
Can Ontario sportsbooks advertise promotions?+
No — not publicly. AGCO regulations prohibit Ontario-licensed sportsbooks from advertising bonuses, free bets, or promotional offers in public-facing marketing. Operators may communicate offers directly to registered, consenting players within their own platform. This is why you won't see bonus comparisons on this site — AGCO-compliant affiliates cannot promote incentives to potential players.
